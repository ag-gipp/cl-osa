The great theme which he discussed in his weighty volumes was "the dangers which come from superstition and despotism and the blessings which flow from the maintenance of religious and political freedom".[FN 1] This theme was, to him, the expression of a universal law which governs "all bodies political as inexorably as Kepler's law controls the motion of planets. The law is Progress; the result Democracy".[FN 2] [FN 1: John L. Motley, History of the United Netherlands from the Death of William the Silent to the Twelve Years' Truce, vol. 1, London, 1901, p. iv.] [FN 2: John L. Motley, Democracy, the Climax of Political Progress and the Destiny of Advanced Races: An Historical Essay, London, 1869, p. 6.]
