Long, static shots are seldom discussed as examples of ‘progressive’ style. Yet, as we saw with the example from INGEBORG HOLM, the long take could be expressive. [...] [...] The opening scene of the German film DER STUDENT VON PRAG (‘The Student of Prague’, 1913, Stellan Rye) consists of a single long take, staged in depth with reframing camera movements and interrupted by several expository intertitles. Balduin enters and sits dejectedly in the foreground, waving his friends away.
