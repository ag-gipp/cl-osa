Indicative of the American antipathy to power and government is the virtual absence of the concept of "the state" in American thought. In its modern form, the idea of the state originated in the fifteenth and sixteenth centuries [...]. Machiavelli, Hobbes and Bodin were its prophets, [...]. The idea of the state implied the concentration of sovereignty in a single, centralized, governmental authority. [...] Two decades later, Bryce declared that the "Americans had no theory of the State and felt no need for one ... The nation is nothing but so many individuals. The government is nothing but certain representatives and officials."
