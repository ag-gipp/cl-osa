As early as December 1915, the trade press reported the popularity of field cinemas and argued implicitly that film entertainment was important for the morale not only of the working class recruits, who had frequented movies before the war, but also for that of the educated officers who might previously have scorned cinema. Film companies supplied feature films to the nine hundred field cinemas in BUFA’s network by March 1917 and advertised to home front exhibitors that they did so.
